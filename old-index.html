Anheuser-Busch Brewery

St. Louis has a long and storied history, and one of the earliest and most prominent businesses in St. Louis was the Anheuser-Busch Brewery. Today, the brewery still stands, open for tourists to gander at the wonder and size of this monumental operation. The best part about touring the brewery is the free beer! However, the famous Clydesdales (and their Dalmation counterparts) are also housed here, and come in a close second.

Blueberry Hill

Located in the Delmar Loop, Blueberry Hill is a famous restaurant and concert venue. Many famous artists have played here, including the legendary Chuck Berry.

Broadway Oyster Bar

A staple of downtown, Broadway Oyster Bar is a New Orleans-style bar and restaurant that is perfect for hippies, classy people, sports fans, and just about everyone!

Busch Stadium

One of the most iconic places in St. Louis...I've seen so many amazing games here and had so much fun with friends and family. It is truly a magical place. Or as some people call it, "Baseball Heaven".

Cahokia Mounds

One of the biggest settlements on the Mississippi, Cahokia Mounds onced housed over a million Native Americans. Today, people are allowed to climb up the mounds and witness the absolutely spectacular view of the surrounding area.

Castlewood State Park

This state park has always held a special place in my heart. Its breathtaking views represent the classic, majestic Midwest.

Cathedral Basilica

One of St. Louis' most iconic worship places. The Cathedral Basilica is a wonder to behold, with its intricate artwork, and amazing style.

Forest Park

Recently voted the best park in any United States City, Forest Park is an epic and expansive park that houses beautiful works by world-reknowned artists, endangered species, live-action Shakespeare performances and more!

World Bird Sanctuary

The birds here are incredible, and to have such a wholesome organization right outside the city limits is truly remarkable!

August A. Busch Conservation Area

This conservation area is unlike any others. Not only does it have over 30 lakes, it also houses bunkers that once kept highly explosive dynamite!

Honrable Mentions

Germany

Across two different trips, I've spent a total of 6 weeks in Germany. My first trip was a month-long backpacking excursion in 2003 that took my travel companion and I through most major regions of the country. We spent time in Heidelberg, Mahneim, Munich, Berlin, Hamburg, Düsseldorf, Köln (Cologne), and Frankfurt.

I returned in 2009 to spend two weeks visiting my friend and college roommate in Munich, where he was working as a physicist.

New York, NY

I never occupied a residence in New York City, but I spend countless days in the city while living on Long Island. The days were an even mix of trips to the city to take classes at Columbia University (where my advisor had a visiting position), seeing live music, and visiting friends in Brooklyn and Queens.
